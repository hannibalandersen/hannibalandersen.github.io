About

Hannibal Andersen creates images, objects and interventions addressing the role of economics in society. Exploring themes such as money, debt, property relations and the implications of relentless privatization, his works inquire wryly into the authority and invisible power of capital and its ideological crutch.

His work has been exhibited widely in Denmark and abroad, including at P.A.D. Projects in New York, MAXXI in Rome, PET Projects in Athens and Vermillion Sands and Kunsthal Charlottenborg in Copenhagen.
